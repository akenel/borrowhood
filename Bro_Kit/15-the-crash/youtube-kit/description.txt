What happens when a rented drone crashes? Deposits, disputes, and rebuttals -- the financial safety net that protects both sides.

Act 1: Sally rents Pietro's DJI Mini 4 Pro. EUR 100 deposit held. She picks it up, flies it, returns it with a cracked gimbal. Pietro files a dispute. Sally responds: wind gust, not negligence. Resolution: partial deposit -- EUR 70 to Pietro for repairs, EUR 30 back to Sally.

Act 2: Sally still can't delete her account -- she has an active service quote from Episode 14. She cancels the quote, tries again, and this time the account deletes. Profile gone. Listings paused. Clean exit.

Two stories. One video. The full lifecycle of money and membership.

How deposits work:
- Deposit collected when rental starts (HELD status)
- Released back to renter on successful return
- Forfeited to owner if item is damaged or lost
- Partial release splits the deposit -- damage cost deducted, remainder returned
- Every deposit links to a rental, a payer, and a recipient

How disputes work:
- Either party files a dispute (reason + description + evidence)
- Other party responds with their side (the rebuttal)
- Status moves to UNDER_REVIEW
- Resolution options: full refund, partial refund, deposit forfeited, deposit returned, no action, account warning, account suspended
- Resolution updates the deposit status automatically

Account deletion safety gates:
- Active rentals (as renter or owner) block deletion
- Held deposits block deletion
- Open disputes block deletion
- Active service quotes block deletion
- Resolve everything first, then delete cleanly

Stack: FastAPI, SQLAlchemy, PostgreSQL, Jinja2, Alpine.js, Tailwind CSS.
