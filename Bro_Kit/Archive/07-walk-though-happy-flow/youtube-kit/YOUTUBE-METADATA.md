BorrowHood turns every garage into a rental shop. No platform fees. No middlemen. Just neighbors sharing tools, equipment, and skills.

This video walks through a complete rental from start to finish. Two neighbors -- Sally and Mike -- go through every step of borrowing a drill. No voiceover, just the app doing its thing. Watch the screens and follow the story.

Here is what happens, step by step:

SALLY NEEDS A DRILL
Sally is building a deck this weekend. She needs a drill but does not own one. She opens BorrowHood and browses the neighborhood. She sees listings from neighbors -- tents, welders, kitchen gear, power tools. She finds Mike's Bosch Professional Drill for EUR 8 per day with a EUR 30 deposit.

SALLY REQUESTS THE RENTAL
Sally logs in with her account. She goes to the drill's page and clicks "Rent This Item." A form pops up. She picks her dates (when she wants to pick it up, when she will return it). She types a message to Mike: "Hi Mike! I'm building a new deck this weekend and need a good drill. I'll take great care of it. Can pick up Friday morning." She clicks "Send Request." Done. The request is now Pending.

MIKE APPROVES IT
Mike gets a notification. He opens his dashboard, goes to "Incoming Requests," and sees Sally's message. He clicks Approve. The rental is now Approved.

MIKE GENERATES LOCKBOX CODES
Here is where it gets interesting. Mike does not need to be home. He generates two access codes -- a pickup code and a return code. He also types instructions: "Garden shed, left shelf, behind the mower. Side gate code: 4571. Drill is in the red case." Sally gets the pickup code on her dashboard. No meetup needed. No scheduling. Just a code and a shed.

SALLY PICKS UP THE DRILL
Sally goes to Mike's garden shed, enters the gate code, finds the drill in the red case, and enters the pickup code on her phone to confirm. The rental status changes to Picked Up.

SALLY RETURNS THE DRILL
Sally finishes her deck. She puts the drill back in the red case, returns it to the shed, and enters the return code on her phone. The rental status changes to Returned.

MIKE COMPLETES THE RENTAL
Mike checks his shed. Drill is there, in the red case. He clicks Complete on his dashboard. Sally's EUR 30 deposit is automatically released back to her. Both earn reputation points and badges.

THAT IS THE WHOLE FLOW
1. Sally found the drill on Browse
2. She requested the rental with dates and a message
3. Mike approved it from his dashboard
4. Mike generated lockbox codes (no meetup needed)
5. Sally picked it up using the code
6. Sally returned it when she was done
7. Mike completed the rental
8. Deposit auto-released back to Sally
9. Both earned reputation points and badges

BONUS: COMMUNITY FEATURES
After the rental story, you also see:
- Workshop profiles -- every user is a "workshop" with skills, languages, items, and reputation
- Members page -- 34 members across Trapani, searchable by city, badge tier, and workshop type
- Help Board -- neighbors asking for help or offering skills (need a ladder, free baking lessons, welding classes)
- Full Italian translation -- 476 strings, one click switches everything

Tech: FastAPI, Keycloak OIDC, Alpine.js, Tailwind CSS, Docker, Hetzner Cloud
Built from a camper van in Trapani, Sicily.

"Every neighborhood has a garage like his."
