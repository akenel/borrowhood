Dave has a Mountain Bike. Sally wants it. So does Rosa.

How do you handle competitive bidding in a community sharing platform?

Build an auction system.

We just shipped the full auction engine for BorrowHood -- a community sharing platform we're building in Sicily.

Here is how it works:
-- Dave lists his Specialized Rockhopper as an auction (EUR 50 starting bid, EUR 5 increment)
-- He sets a hidden reserve price (EUR 120) -- the minimum he'll accept
-- Sally sees the auction with a live countdown timer and places the first bid
-- Rosa outbids her at EUR 75 -- Sally gets a notification instantly
-- Sally comes back at EUR 130 -- reserve price met, checkmark appears
-- Dave ends the auction -- Sally wins the bike

Anti-snipe protection: bid in the last 60 seconds and the clock extends by 2 minutes. No sniping.

Every bid updates the panel in real time. Outbid notifications. Reserve met indicator. Countdown with days, hours, minutes, seconds. Owner ends it when ready.

Watch the 3-minute demo. Every click is real, running on a live Hetzner server.
