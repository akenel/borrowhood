What happens when a rented drone crashes? Five acts. Two neighbors. One deposit that changes everything.

Sally rents Pietro's DJI Mini 4 Pro for aerial shots in Scopello. EUR 50 rental + EUR 100 deposit = EUR 150 total. She picks it up, flies it over the cliffs, and returns it with a cracked gimbal and a snapped propeller.

Pietro files a dispute. Sally responds: wind gust, not negligence. The platform tracks both sides. Resolution: EUR 70 to Pietro for repairs, EUR 30 back to Sally. Fair. Done.

But then something unexpected happens.

Pietro browses Sally's workshop and finds her 200-piece cookie cutter collection. His niece Sofia's birthday is next week. He rents the cookie cutters as a peace offering. Sally approves -- and throws in extra shapes for Sofia.

A crashed drone brought them together. Now the question is: Sally bakes the best cookies in Trapani but can't deliver. Pietro has a licensed drone. Johnny Abela's bike is always broken.

What if the drone guy... delivered the cookies?

How deposits work in BorrowHood:
- Deposit collected at rental start (HELD)
- Released on successful return
- Forfeited on damage or loss
- Partial split when both sides have a case

How disputes work:
- Either party files (reason + description)
- Other party submits rebuttal
- Resolution options: full refund, partial, forfeited, returned
- Deposit updated automatically
