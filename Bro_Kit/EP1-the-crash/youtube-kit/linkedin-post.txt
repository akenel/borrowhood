What happens when a rented drone crashes?

That's the pilot episode of BorrowHood Season 1 -- a 12-minute demo built entirely with Claude Code (Opus 4.6), running on a cheap Hetzner server.

Five acts. Two neighbors. One deposit.

Sally rents Pietro's drone for EUR 150 (fee + deposit). She crashes it. He files a dispute. She responds. The platform splits the deposit: EUR 70 for repairs, EUR 30 back to Sally.

Then something unexpected happens. Pietro rents Sally's cookie cutter collection for his niece Sofia's birthday. The peace offering. From there, a question emerges:

Sally bakes the best cookies in Trapani but can't deliver.
Pietro has a licensed drone.

What if the drone guy delivered the cookies?

That's not just a feature demo. That's a business partnership born from a dispute resolution system.

Tech stack: FastAPI, PostgreSQL, Keycloak OIDC, Alpine.js, Tailwind CSS
Production: Puppeteer (non-headless) + OBS Screen Capture + ffmpeg
AI: Claude Code (Opus 4.6) -- co-pilot from script to deployment
Cost: peanuts (Hetzner CX32)
