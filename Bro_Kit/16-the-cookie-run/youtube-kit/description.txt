What happens when a 17-year-old bakes her first cookies and needs to deliver them across town?

Previously: Sally rented Pietro's drone. It crashed. Dispute filed. Deposit split EUR 70/30. Then Pietro rented Sally's cookie cutters -- for his niece Sofia's birthday. The question was: "What if the drone guy... delivered the cookies?"

This is that episode.

Sofia Ferretti is 17. She just got 200 cookie cutters from her uncle Pietro. Sally Thompson -- cookie queen, trusted member -- is her baking mentor. Their first session: almond biscuits from scratch. Six burned. Four perfect. "You learn from the ones that burn."

Sofia creates her first commission listing: birthday cookie boxes. EUR 15 each. Five orders come in. Pietro orders first. Then neighbors. Then friends.

Problem: 5 boxes, 5 addresses, no delivery. Enter Johnny Abela -- the delivery kid with the broken bike. Chain held together with zip ties. Frame bent from a pothole on Via Fardella. He loads one box, rides 200 meters, chain snaps. Cookies on the ground.

Pietro walks in. "I have a drone." The DJI Mini 4 Pro. Cookie box strapped underneath. First delivery: success. Customer films it. Posts it on Instagram. By the fifth delivery, people on the street are pointing at the sky.

All 5 delivered. Sofia made EUR 50 profit. But Johnny is standing there with his broken bike. He did nothing. He was supposed to be the delivery kid.

The drone can fly but can't knock on doors. Johnny knocks on doors but can't fly. Sally bakes but can't deliver.

Sally's cookie sales this week: EUR 235. A used electric scooter: EUR 200.

She sees his face.

What if the cookie queen... bought the delivery kid his wheels?

How mentorships work in BorrowHood:
- Mentor teaches, apprentice learns
- Three types: mentor, apprentice, intern
- Platform tracks the relationship
- Mentors earn reputation, apprentices earn skills

How commissions work:
- List a custom item or service
- Customers place orders with messages
- Creator fulfills and ships (or in this case, flies)
