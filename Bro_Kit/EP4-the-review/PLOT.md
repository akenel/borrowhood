## The Theme

Johnny's pressure washer broke. The trigger is stuck, the pump leaks. It's all
plastic -- he thinks it's trash. He's about to drag it to the curb and wait for
big garbage day. But then he stops. Last time he gave away the bike for free and
Leo turned it into a EUR 50 sale. He's not making that mistake twice.

EP4 is the episode where the Help Board comes alive. Where Johnny learns that
posting "my pressure washer trigger is broken" gets him an answer in hours --
not a new machine, but a EUR 0.50 gasket, a pocket knife, and 20 minutes in
the field. The AI tells him the washer is worth EUR 80-120 used. The fix is
trivial. Don't trash it, don't give it away. Ask first.

The deeper lesson: the stuff people throw out isn't broken. It's one gasket,
one weld, one YouTube video away from working. The Help Board is where the
neighborhood saves each other from the landfill.

And Leo? He's been doing this his whole life. He picks up the free stuff,
fixes it with spare parts that cost him nothing, and sells or rents it back.
The Help Board IS his inventory pipeline. The giveaway page IS his supply chain.
That's how a Legend makes money on BorrowHood -- not by buying, but by KNOWING.

The excuses people make:
- "Nobody wants it" -- the AI tells you it's worth EUR 80
- "I can't deliver it" -- Johnny delivers on his bike, or use the lockbox
- "Too much work to post" -- the AI writes the whole post for you
- "I'd have to explain myself" -- the Help Board is one paragraph and a photo
- "Strangers knocking on my door" -- lockbox, no contact needed

Every excuse has an answer. The platform kills the friction. This episode
shows what happens when someone stops making excuses and just asks.

### ACT I -- THE FALL (Scenes 4-12)

**Johnny's pressure washer breaks. He almost throws it out.**

4. Login as Johnny -- dashboard shows his delivery history, 8 completed orders
5. Card: JOHNNY'S PROBLEM -- His pressure washer trigger is stuck and the pump leaks. It's all plastic. He thinks it's done. Big garbage day is Thursday. He's already picturing it on the curb.
6. Johnny opens Browse, looks at the "FREE" giveaway option. He's about to list it as trash for pickup.
7. STOP. Card: "LAST TIME HE GAVE AWAY THE BIKE FOR FREE. LEO SOLD IT FOR EUR 50. NOT THIS TIME."
8. Johnny clicks Help Board in the nav. First time he's been here.
9. He sees the board -- summary cards (needs, offers, resolved), filter pills, search
10. Johnny clicks "New Post" -- the create modal opens. He stares at the empty title field. Doesn't know what to write. "Pressure washer broken?" Sounds dumb.
11. **AI DRAFT** -- Johnny clicks the AI assist button. Types: "pressure washer trigger stuck pump leaking all plastic". The AI comes back:
    - Title: "Need Help: Pressure Washer -- Trigger Stuck & Pump Leaking"
    - Body: 3 paragraphs about the situation, common fix (gasket replacement), what tools he'd need
    - Category: home_improvement
    - **Estimated value: EUR 80-120** (working condition used price)
    - **Value explanation**: "A working pressure washer sells for EUR 80-120 used. Trigger and pump gasket failures are the #1 repair -- usually a EUR 0.50 gasket and 20 minutes with a pocket knife. Don't throw this out."
    - Suggestions: ["Check if someone on the platform has spare gaskets", "A pocket knife and a flat-head screwdriver is all you need", "Tag the item so helpers can see the model"]
12. Johnny reads it. EUR 0.50 in parts. Twenty minutes. He was about to throw out an EUR 80 machine over a gasket. He edits the draft, adds a photo of the leaking pump, hits Post.

### ACT II -- THE RESPONSE (Scenes 13-22)

**The community responds. Reviews come alive.**

13. Card: "24 HOURS LATER"
14. Login as Leo -- he opens the Help Board first thing. This is his morning routine. He's not browsing -- he's SCOUTING. He checks "Needs" filter, sorts by newest. This is how a Legend finds inventory: people post problems, Leo sees opportunity.
15. Leo spots Johnny's post. Photo of the leaking pump. He knows EXACTLY what this is. Clicks Reply: "Johnny, that's a Karcher K-series. The trigger assembly pops off with a flat-head. The pump gasket is a standard O-ring -- I have a bag of 50 in my Bottega. Come by tomorrow, we'll fix it in the field in 20 minutes. Bring a pocket knife."
    - Card: LEO'S BUSINESS MODEL -- Leo picks up free stuff, fixes it with parts that cost him nothing, sells or rents it. He doesn't buy inventory. He reads the Help Board. The giveaway page is his supply chain. That's how a Legend operates.
16. Login as Mike -- sees the Help Board. Posts his own OFFER: "I Can Help With: Power Tool & Equipment Safety"
    - Body: "30 years in the garage. I've seen what happens when you skip the safety check. If you're renting a pressure washer, chipper, or saw for the first time -- message me. Free. No appointment. And for the love of God, don't stick your arm in the chipper."
    - Category: home_improvement
    - Urgency: low (standing offer)
17. Mike sees Johnny's post. Clicks Reply: "Johnny, the pump gasket is 50 cents at any hardware store. But check the unloader valve too -- if that's stuck, the trigger won't release pressure. I can walk you through it. And next time -- POST HERE FIRST before throwing things out."
18. Johnny edits his post -- updates the body with "Leo has the gasket! Going to his Bottega tomorrow. Mike says check the unloader valve too." (Shows the EDIT feature working)
19. Card: "NEXT DAY -- LEO'S BOTTEGA"
20. Pressure washer fixed (off-screen, implied). New gasket, trigger works, pump sealed. Total cost: EUR 0. 20 minutes. Johnny logs in. Goes to his Help Board post.
21. Johnny clicks "Mark as Resolved" -- picks Leo as the helper (Leo had the gasket).
22. Leo gets the notification: "Johnny marked you as the helper!" Leo's profile now shows: "Helped 1 neighbor."

### ACT III -- THE REVIEWS (Scenes 23-38)

**Sofia reviews Johnny. Leo reviews Sofia. Pietro reads them all.**

23. Card: "THE REVIEWS" (dramatic)
24. Login as Sofia -- dashboard, completed orders tab, she sees her delivered cookie orders
25. Sofia opens a completed order (Johnny delivered). Clicks "Leave Review."
26. **FULL REVIEW FLOW:**
    - Consent checkbox (Sofia checks it)
    - 5 stars overall
    - Subcategories: Accuracy 5, Communication 5, Value 5, Timeliness 5
    - "Would recommend?" -- YES (thumbs up)
    - Body: "Johnny delivered my cookies in 20 minutes, still warm. He even asked if the customer was happy before leaving. If you need delivery in Trapani, Johnny is your man. He deserves every star."
    - Submits
27. Card: DELIVERY TRACKING -- we show the tracking timeline for a past order
28. Login as Leo -- Leo has a completed cookie order from Sofia (the drone one)
29. Leo writes his review: 3 stars overall
    - Subcategories: Accuracy 5, Communication 2, Value 5, Timeliness 1
    - "Would recommend?" -- YES (despite the score)
    - Body: "Best cookies in Trapani. But if Johnny delivers them, get the bike option. The drone left mine on the balcony like a seagull dropping a fish. Cookies were cold 3 hours later."
30. Sofia sees the notification. Her rating drops to 4.0. She panics.
31. Login as Sally -- Sally shows Sofia the review. "Read what he ACTUALLY said. The cookies are perfect. The delivery method was the problem."
32. **OWNER RESPONSE** -- Sofia responds to Leo's review: "Leo, thank you! Johnny is our best delivery rider. We've disabled drone delivery for now -- your cookies deserve a human touch."
    - Show the review LOCK -- Leo can't edit his review now. Fair for both sides.
33. Login as Pietro -- Pietro reads Leo's review on the item page
34. **STAR DISTRIBUTION** -- Show the star distribution chart: one 5-star (Sofia's review of Johnny? No -- this is on Sofia's cookies). One 5-star, one 3-star. The chart is visual.
35. Pietro clicks "Helpful" on Leo's review (thumbs up). The helpful count goes to 1.
36. Pietro writes his own review: 5 stars. "I always pick Johnny delivery. Cookies arrive warm. RTFM on the delivery options, people."
    - "Would recommend?" -- YES
37. Recommendation rate: 100% (all 3 reviewers said yes, even the 3-star guy)
38. **BREADCRUMBS** -- Show a new user (George?) landing on Sofia's cookies via Browse. Click through: Home / Food & Drinks / Sofia's Sweet Kitchen / Nonna's Chocolate Cookies. Click back to workshop, see all items. Flow like water.

### EPILOGUE (Scenes 39-42)

39. Login as Johnny -- dashboard
40. Johnny opens the Help Board. His post is marked RESOLVED. Leo's reply. Mike's reply. The photo of the leaking pump. The AI told him EUR 80-120. Leo fixed it with a EUR 0.50 gasket. He was about to throw out an EUR 80 machine. Over a rubber ring.
